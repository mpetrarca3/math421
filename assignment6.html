#The density plot for the variable 'Mileage' demonstrates that there is a high concentration of cars with 60,000-100,000 miles in the secondary market. There are practically 0 cars in the market with a mileage of over 300,000 miles.

df %>% ggplot()+
  geom_density(mapping = aes(x = Mileage))

#The histogram of the variable `Mileage` reinforces the pattern observed from the density plot. There is a higher frequency of cars on the secondary market with a mileage between 60,000 and 100,000 miles. There are no cars on the market after 500,000 miles.

df %>% ggplot()+
  geom_histogram(mapping = aes(x = Mileage))

#The boxplot for the variable `Mileage` indicates the average mileage for a car on the used car market is approximately 80,000 miles (from the vertical line). From the width of the white box, the interquartile range is approximately 60,000 to 120,000 miles. Most of the outliers exceed 120,000 miles.

df %>% ggplot()+
  geom_boxplot(mapping = aes(x = Mileage))

#The density plot of the variables `Mileage` and `Transmission Type` displays that there are more automatic cars with lower mileage (below 100,000 miles) than manual cars. After exceeding 100,000 miles, there are more manual cars than automatic cars. This is can be explained by automatic vehicles becoming much more common today than ever before. Today, there are few companies that produce vehicles with manual transmission.

df %>% ggplot()+
  geom_density(mapping = aes(x = `Mileage`, color = `Transmission Type`))

#From the boxplot, the average mileage for a car with manual transmission is greater than for a car with automatic transmission. Due to the narrower box for automatic cars, the interquartile range for automatic cars is smaller indicating that there is less variance between `Mileage` and cars with automatic transition.

df %>% ggplot()+
  geom_boxplot(mapping = aes(x = Mileage, y = `Transmission Type`))

  1. For two categorical variables: barplot

In the automatic category, the body types 'Cross Over' and 'SUV' increased greatly between the two classes of transmission. The portion of 'Hatchback' declined from manual to automatic transition. These changes between categories indicates that there is a time element involved. Crossovers and SUVs weren't produced as much in the era of manual transition.
